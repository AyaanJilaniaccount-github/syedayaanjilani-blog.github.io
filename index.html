Its the first principles thinking in practice is what I aim for.I will not hesitate to question the root itself if it doesnt hold up.
I sort of have felt this tuning with this field of cybersecurity.This personal interest and outlook on my life is applied in reverse engineering, because - to me it is about rebuilding the entire codebase structure from the roots and all the way to the top. Binary is essentially the raw version that actually works in practice in computing.It can be defined as the very base on which modern day computing takes place.

All this code, database, tokens, signatures and so forth are just... a bunch of on and off signals at its foundation.These are the two states arbitarily noted as 1's and 0's
It might seem boring at first to some and thats totally understandable why it is so.After all it is just a bunch of on and off states.However, it is quite interesting how certain engineers have been able to really use these two states into calculations and convert it into mathematics itself.
Mathematics is important here.Mathematics to me is the attempt of humanity to understand the observable universe and beyond with a systematic order of numbers.In this case these are binary numbers.

So coming back to cybersecurity...
I believe that it is important to understand the basics of code itself - and I have typed in the previous lines alot but I feel like emphasizing even more often.The tools, frameworks, systems are all just mathematical calculations at its base that simplify this process of analysis.
What I consider to be important is not only the application of recalling but also a sort of intuitive pattern recognition.To me intuition in a generic sense is something that one can form connections with past experiences and what they currently observe and derive decently accurate assumptions on something that can be later verified by deconstruction and evidence based proof.
What I also consider to be important especially in cybersecurity is to not have rigidity of thought.One should not fixate on something that is completely disregarding other perspectives which can also have some weight and validity.This objectivity is immensely important as it allows one to really see the intent behind a particular design - and the flaws in that design.Cybersecurity also happens to be a thing of psychology significantly alongside the code.
There is a quite popular saying in this field that humans are often the weakest link in a cybersecurity structural design practice.That does have weight although this does not mean that I am advocating for blind, unregulated artificial intelligence usage.However, when building or breaking a code system one should absolutely take the mind involved in perspective.
This can be the mind of the user, mind of the developer, mind of the management, mind of the designer and so forth.The list can really go quite long but the conclusion is that code is done for human beings.It is meant to be used directly or indirectly for human beings.It makes sense that the human thought is to be taken into consideration when looking at any codebase.
Cybersecurity is also having, interestingly, a good amount of mathematics involved.Whether it be CPU registers or hexadecimal representation of addresses in memory, or just segments in RAM,obfuscation algorithms, cryptography - the list goes on and on.
